\paragraph{Cognitive maps in the Transformer and RNN literature.} The
Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine (TEM)~\citep{whittington2020tem} and its transformer
variant TEM-T~\citep{whittington2022tem-t} train on many random graph
environments to learn abstract relational structure. MapFormer
\citep{rambaud2025mapformer} inherits this setup but uses input-dependent
positional rotations in place of TEM's recurrent weights.

\paragraph{Positional encodings.} RoPE~\citep{su2021roformer} applies fixed
token-index rotations; CoPE~\citep{golovneva2024cope} replaces token indices
with content-based soft counts; TAPE~\citep{zhu2025tape} uses input-dependent
MLPs after attention. The MapFormer paper's Table 2 shows all three fail on
2D grid navigation at 1 layer (RoPE 0.16, CoPE 0.74, TAPE 0.32 IID).
MapFormer-WM $\approx 1.00$.

\paragraph{Parallel Bayesian filters.} \citet{sarkka2021parallel} show
parallelizable Bayesian filters via associative scan. Mamba~\citep{gu2024mamba}
brought such scans to deep learning. Our parallel InEKF is a specific instance,
specialised to $\mathrm{SO}(2)$ with scalar affine scans.

\paragraph{Invariant extended Kalman filtering.} \citet{barrau2017inekf} is
the canonical reference. \citet{markovic2017wrapping} shows that on
$\mathrm{SO}(2)$, the Lie-Group EKF equals an EKF with $\mathrm{atan2}$-wrapped
innovation --- the identity we exploit to keep Level 1.5 simple.

\paragraph{Grid-cell modeling.} Grid cells fire in hexagonal patterns
\citep{hafting2005grid,sargolini2006conjunctive} organised in discrete
modules with $\sqrt{2}$-ratio spacing \citep{stensola2012modules}. We
compare in Section~\ref{sec:results}.
