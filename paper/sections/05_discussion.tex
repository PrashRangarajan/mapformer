Level 1.5 is a specialised Mamba-style selective-scan on $\mathrm{SO}(2)$.
The \citet{wang2025mamba-kalman} view (Mamba $\equiv$ linear Kalman filter)
tells us that selective SSMs \emph{can} implement Kalman filters. Level 1.5
shows that they \emph{should}, at least on tasks with known geometric
structure, because the specialisation (Kalman-ratio gain, Lie-group wrap,
decoupled path-integration scan) yields a large improvement in what the
model can discover from gradient descent at limited training scale.

\paragraph{Honest limitations.} Single-environment training (each model
trains on one fixed obs\_map); single-scale training (we follow MapFormer's
1 layer, 2 heads, $d{=}128$); single Lie group ($\mathrm{SO}(2)$ only); and
hippocampal correspondence is only partially supported (we do not produce
hexagonal grid cells due to an architectural constraint described in
Section~\ref{sec:future}).

\paragraph{When to use Level 1.5.} (1) If the environment is clean and
fully aliased, MapFormer is already at ceiling --- use Level 1.5 only for
calibrated uncertainty. (2) If actions are noisy or the environment has
landmarks, Level 1.5 adds +11\,pp at $T{=}512$ OOD. (3) If you need
calibrated uncertainty for downstream planning, Level 1.5 is strictly
preferable on any task.

\paragraph{Broader direction.} Constrained Mamba architectures with explicit
group-equivariance. SO(2) is the simplest case; SE(2) and SE(3) generalise
to 2D/3D navigation; other compact Lie groups cover other periodic
structures. In each case the specialised Mamba block would look like
Level 1.5 but with Adjoint-weighted scans and higher-dimensional
measurement models. We expect the discoverability advantage to persist.
