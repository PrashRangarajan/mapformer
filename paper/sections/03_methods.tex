\subsection{Notation and Task}

Following~\citet{rambaud2025mapformer}, we work on a 2D torus grid of size
$N{=}64$ with $K{=}16$ aliased observation types and $p_\mathrm{empty}=0.5$.
The agent emits an interleaved trajectory
$s = (a_1, o_1, a_2, o_2, \ldots, a_T, o_T)$ over random walks ($k$ steps per
chosen direction, $k \in \{1,\ldots,10\}$). Loss is next-token cross-entropy
applied only at \emph{revisited} observation positions.

\subsection{MapFormer path integration}

Path integration uses an input-dependent SO(2) rotation. For each block
$b \in \{1,\ldots,n_b\}$ with learnable angular velocity $\omega_b$:
\begin{equation}
  \theta_{\mathrm{path},t}^{(b)} = \omega_b \cdot \sum_{s=1}^{t} \Delta_s^{(b)},
  \qquad
  \Delta_s = W_\Delta^{\mathrm{out}}\, W_\Delta^{\mathrm{in}}\, x_s
  \label{eq:path-int}
\end{equation}
The cumulative sum~\eqref{eq:path-int} is $\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ via parallel
scan. The geometric initialisation is $\omega_b = 2\pi \cdot (1/N)^{b/(n_b-1)}$.

\subsection{Level 1.5 InEKF on SO(2)}

Add three learnable components: a constant log-prior covariance
$\log\Pi \in \mathbb{R}^{n_h \times n_b}$, a per-token log-measurement noise
$\log R_t$ from an MLP, and a measurement model
$z_t \in [-\pi,\pi]^{n_h \times n_b}$ from an MLP:
\begin{equation}
  z_t = \pi \cdot \tanh\!\big(f_z(x_t)\big), \qquad
  \log R_t = f_R(x_t)
\end{equation}
Wrapped innovation and Kalman gain:
\begin{equation}
  \nu_t = \mathrm{atan2}\!\big(\sin(z_t - \theta_{\mathrm{path},t}), \,
                                \cos(z_t - \theta_{\mathrm{path},t})\big),
  \qquad
  K_t = \frac{\Pi}{\Pi + R_t}
\end{equation}
Affine correction scan ($\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ Hillis-Steele):
\begin{equation}
  d_t = (1 - K_t)\, d_{t-1} + K_t \cdot \nu_t,
  \qquad
  \hat\theta_t = \theta_{\mathrm{path},t} + d_t
\end{equation}
The corrected $\hat\theta_t$ feeds MapFormer's input-dependent rotation in
place of $\theta_{\mathrm{path},t}$.

\subsection{Level 1.5-EM: safe init}

For the MapFormer-EM backbone (Hadamard product attention
$\mathrm{softmax}(A_X \odot A_P)\,V$), default initialisation of $\log R$ at
zero gives $K{=}0.5$ at start. This corrupts $A_P$, which Hadamard-products
with $A_X$ to destroy gradient signal; 3 of 9 seeds catastrophically diverge.
We initialise $\log R_\mathrm{head}$ with bias $3.0$, giving $K{\approx}0.05$
at start (InEKF behaves as a near-no-op at init, then learns $R$ downward
where measurements are informative). This is the standard residual-addition
safe-init trick.

\subsection{Training}

AdamW, lr $3{\times}10^{-4}$, weight decay 0.05, linear LR decay, batch size
128, 50 epochs $\times$ 156 batches ($\sim$1M sequences), $T{=}128$ training
length. Three configurations: \textbf{clean} (no action noise, no landmarks),
\textbf{noise} (10\% action noise), \textbf{lm200} (10\% noise + 200 unique
landmarks, $\approx$ 5\% density). Three training seeds each.
