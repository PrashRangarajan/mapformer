(model predicts BFS-optimal next action). Chance = 1/4 = 0.250.

## Interpretation

Chance baseline: 0.250 (uniform over 4 actions). BFS-optimal upper
bound depends on whether multiple shortest paths exist (often 2-4
ties on a torus, so a 'tie-aware' upper bound is ~0.7-0.8; strict
BFS-match is what we measure here).

- Vanilla > chance: even without correction, attention extracts enough
  cognitive-map information from the explore phase to action-select.
- Level15 > Vanilla: correction improves goal-directed action selection.
- Level15EM vs Level15: does the multiplicative AND-gate help or hurt
  when goal is content-specified (the landmark emits its unique token)?
- Level15NoDrop vs Level15: does removing post-attn dropout (the
  intervention that won lm200 prediction by +12pp) also win on goal-
  directed action selection?

- T_explore=128 OOD column: tests whether the cognitive map degrades
  with longer explore phases (drift) — exactly the regime where
  Level15 stabilisation matters.
